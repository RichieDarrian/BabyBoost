Tips to Handle Tantrums in Toddler?

Parenting Tips · 2 hours ago · 14 replies · 128 views
A
Adit S.
Posted 2 hours ago
Hi parents, my 3-year-old has been having frequent tantrums lately, especially when things don't go his way. Sometimes he cries, screams, or throws toys, and honestly it can feel really overwhelming.

I've tried calming him down and distracting him, but it doesn't always work. Do you have any tips or experiences on handling toddler tantrums? Thank you!

Replies

14

Staying calm is the single most effective thing I've found. When I match my toddler's energy, things escalate fast. Taking a deep breath and lowering my voice actually helps him come down too. It takes practice but it really works!

R
Rizky F.
8 hours ago

Giving simple choices works wonders for us. Instead of saying "stop crying," I'll ask "do you want juice or water?" It redirects their attention and gives them a sense of control, which is often what they're really after.

A
Aldi T.
8 hours ago

A consistent sleep schedule made a huge difference for us. Overtiredness is a major tantrum trigger that I overlooked for a long time. Since we locked in nap times and bedtime, the frequency dropped noticeably within just two weeks.

Z
Zeta A.
12 hours ago

Sometimes toddlers just want to feel understood first. I started naming the emotion — "I can see you're really frustrated right now" — before trying to fix anything. It's amazing how often just being acknowledged calms them down.

M
Maya K.
14 hours ago

We created a "calm corner" with a soft mat, some favorite stuffed animals, and a feelings chart. When a tantrum starts, I guide him there and tell him it's a safe place to feel big feelings. It took a few weeks to click but now he goes there on his own sometimes!

D
Dina P.
1 day ago

Hunger is such an underrated trigger! I started keeping healthy snacks in my bag wherever we go. Half the time the tantrum just needed a cracker and some water. Never underestimate a hangry toddler!
